## Five-Principle Model

### 1. SEO Enables Retrieval and Attribution

Technical SEO remains the foundation because systems need accessible, readable, attributable content. Prioritize crawlability, indexability, meaningful HTTP behavior, server-rendered core content, stable performance, source clarity, and identifiable authorship.

For Google AI features, follow Google Search Central: normal indexing and snippet eligibility remain necessary. For other AI systems, crawler and retrieval behavior varies and should be tested rather than assumed.

### 2. Entity Clarity Extends Across Sources

Make the company, products, categories, audiences, capabilities, and areas of authority unambiguous in visible content first. Mirror that structure in schema and other machine-readable systems.

Check consistency across:

- canonical website
- product and documentation pages
- Organization and product schema
- LinkedIn, YouTube, GitHub, and other owned profiles
- review platforms and industry directories
- independent coverage and customer references

Schema is a representation of coherent information, not a substitute for it. Do not state that schema directly controls LLM training or citations.

### 3. Important Passages Must Be Extractable

Retrieval-based systems often use specific passages rather than whole pages. Optimize definitions, explanations, comparisons, methods, and key facts so they remain accurate when separated from nearby context.

Prefer:

- one complete idea per important paragraph
- direct definitions and conclusions at the beginning
- descriptive headings that match real questions
- explicit subjects instead of vague references such as "this" or "as above"
- scoped statistics with source, date, denominator, and methodology
- comparison criteria and limitations in the same passage

Do not turn every paragraph into repetitive answer blocks. Preserve natural reading quality and use extractability where it improves clarity for humans.

This guidance is strongest for retrieval-augmented or browsing systems. Do not infer that page formatting immediately changes knowledge stored in a base model.

### 4. Visibility Includes Owned and Earned Presence

Audit useful presence outside the website.

Owned presence may include:

- expert LinkedIn posts or newsletters
- product demonstrations and educational YouTube videos
- webinars, podcasts, conference talks, and research presentations
- GitHub repositories, documentation, and public methodology
- helpful participation in relevant communities

Earned presence may include:

- authentic customer reviews
- independent industry coverage
- expert citations and partner references
- customer-created discussions and recommendations
- inclusion in credible directories or comparative research

Owned material supplies detail; earned material supplies independent corroboration. Never fabricate reviews, community discussion, editorial coverage, or customer endorsements.

Choose channels based on actual audience behavior and source quality. Do not publish low-value duplicate content everywhere merely to create mentions.

### 5. Measure AI Visibility Separately

Traditional search and AI visibility require separate but connected measurement.

Track:

- citation frequency: how often the brand or source is cited
- mention rate: how often the brand appears with or without a link
- competitor share of voice: brand mentions divided by total tracked competitor mentions
- prompt and topic context: which questions trigger or omit the brand
- framing accuracy: whether the product, audience, category, and capabilities are described correctly
- sentiment: positive, neutral, negative, or mixed framing
- cited-source profile: domains and source types used in answers
- source diversity: whether visibility depends on one unstable source
- referral traffic and assisted conversion when observable

Store the exact prompt, platform/model, date, locale, mode, response, rank/order, citations, and competitor mentions. Repeat the same prompt set over time because individual answers and cited sources can fluctuate.

Do not convert a small prompt sample into false precision. Report missing platform coverage, unstable results, and methodology limits.

## Operating Interpretation

Use the five principles as a probability framework:

1. Make the source retrievable.
2. Make the entity understandable.
3. Make the evidence extractable.
4. Reinforce the entity through useful owned and earned sources.
5. Measure repeated visibility and framing rather than expecting a fixed rank.

No individual tactic guarantees a mention or citation. Platform behavior, user context, conversation history, retrieval indexes, and model updates can change the output.

## Recommended Deliverable Structure

For broad GEO strategy, organize recommendations into:

1. Technical retrieval and indexability
2. Entity and category clarity
3. Extractable first-party evidence
4. Owned distribution
5. Earned authority and reputation
6. AI visibility measurement

Label each recommendation as:

- Google-confirmed
- third-party observation
- implementation inference
